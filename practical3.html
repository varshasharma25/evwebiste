Practical 3

Construction of Engineering Curves: Cycloid, Involute & Archimedean Spiral

Theory Overview

Engineering curves are essential for designing various mechanical parts and architectural structures. In this practical, we focus on three fundamental types:

  • Cycloid: The path traced by a point on the circumference of a circle as it rolls along a straight line.
  • Involute: The curve traced by the end of a string as it is unwound from a fixed circle or polygon.
  • Archimedean Spiral: A curve traced by a point moving uniformly along a ray while the ray rotates uniformly about its endpoint (pole).
